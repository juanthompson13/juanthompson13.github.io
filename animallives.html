Animallives.org Case Study

Animallives.org

Animallives.org is a web application developed by Yale and NASA for scientists to track the movement of animal lives around the world. I worked alongside 3 other product designers to identify the animallives.org target audience and build a working high fidelity prototype for the next stage of development. We wore many hats as product designers for this project, working on research, information architecture, UX design, storytelling, and UI design.

Project Overview

Follow the lives of animals in easy, beautiful, and meaningful ways.

Animallives.org aims to present vast animal tracking data in beautiful, organized, and personalized ways to inform policy and scientific research. The Yale Center for Biodiversity and Global Change in conjunction with NASA partnered with user experience collaborators to aid in re-imagining the site.

Client Goals

  • Identify the animallives.org target audience.
  • Create a website centered around animal movement data representations that are clear, credible, aesthetically appealing, and readily actionable by users.

Results

At handoff, we delivered reports, prototypes, and mockups to empower the next stage of development.

Research

Approach

We began by familiarizing ourselves with the wildlife research field and understanding target users, their behaviors, and preferences.

Goals

  • Understand what users want from animallives.org compared to other tools.
  • Identify unmet needs based on user behaviors.

Questions

  • What do stakeholders expect on geo tracking data websites?
  • What geotracking data is most important?
  • What do stakeholders like about competitor tools?
  • How do research methods vary?
  • What difficulties exist in animal tracking research?

Stakeholder Interviews

We interviewed 7 professionals from relevant backgrounds, which shaped our user research and product design.

Affinity Mapping

We grouped participant quotes to identify themes in speech tone, potential features, frustrations, and goals.

Key Findings

  • Few scientists use geo mapping tools like animallives.org or MoveBank.
  • Mixed opinions on citizen science data collection.
  • Storytelling and visual creativity are essential in the design phase.

Competitor Analysis

Usability Testing

Validation

Through Zoom usability tests with prototypes and competitor sites, we observed usability and excitement metrics.

A/B Testing

We tested a new search design that improved data card previews on the map, confirming better user intuition.

Next Steps

Final Recommendations

  • Ensure transparency in data modeling and ethics.
  • Educate users with tooltips, tutorials, and FAQs.
  • Create a welcoming, informative landing page.
  • Optimize performance for fast load times to outpace competitors.
